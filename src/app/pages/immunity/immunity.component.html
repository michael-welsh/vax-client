Herd immunity occurs when a large portion of the population becomes immune to a disease, either through vaccination or by developing antibodies to the disease. The spread of the disease from person to person then becomes more unlikely.

The Threshold is the percentage of the population that is immune to the disease. If the number of immune people surpasses this threshold, the disease will begin to decline. This percentage can be hard to define and varies from disease to disease. For COVID-19, no specific percentage has currently been accepted by the Hong Kong government, making this results of this graph hypothetical.

The Measurement is an average either taken of the last seven days, or since the time COVID-19 vaccinations began in Hong Kong. This too is an imprecise measurement, as we can only speculate on the willingness and/or hesitancy of the Hong Kong public to receive the vaccination moving forward.
